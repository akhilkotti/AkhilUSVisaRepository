US Visa Dropbox Documents Submission Guide

Here is exactly what you need to bring and what to expect at the visa center.

1. Mandatory Document Checklist

Every applicant (including infants) must have these documents in their own dedicated stack/folder:

  • Current Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay.
  • Old Passports: Specifically the one containing your most recent US B1/B2 visa.
  • Appointment Confirmation Letter: This is the letter generated by the US Visa Scheduling portal after you book your slot.
    • Note: You can carry the same "Appointment Confirmation" letter for all, but it’s best to have a copy for each folder.
  • DS-160 Confirmation Page: Ensure the barcode is clear and crisp.
    • Note: If you updated your DS-160 after booking the appointment, you must bring both the original confirmation page used to book the slot and the new "corrected" one.
  • Payment Receipt: While usually linked to your profile, carrying the MRV fee payment proof is a safe backup.
    • Note: Having a copy of the MRV receipt in each folder is highly recommended.
  • Two Physical Photos: Two, 2x2 inch (51x51 mm) color photos.
    • Note: Ensure the photos haven't been used for any previous visa.

Photo Standards

The Official Standard: The U.S. Department of State officially defines the photo size as exactly 2 inches x 2 inches.

In practice, if you go to a professional studio and ask for a "US Visa Photo," they will use a standard template that is 2x2 inches. As long as the physical photo is a perfect square of 2 inches, it will be accepted regardless of whether the studio's ruler calls it 50 mm or 51 mm.

Aside from the outer dimensions, a few "make-or-break" rules you must follow:

  • No Glasses: Eye glasses have not been allowed since November 1, 2016.
  • Recency: The photo must be taken within the last six months and reflect your current appearance.
  • No "Recycling": If you are renewing, the photo must not be the same one used on your previous visa.
  • Head Position: Your head height must occupy 50% to 70% of the total photo height.

2. What Happens at the Visa Application Center (VAC)?

The process at the center is usually quick (15–30 minutes) and does not involve an interview with a consular officer.

  1. Security Check: You will undergo a security screening. Avoid bringing large bags, phones, or laptops.
  2. Initial Verification: Staff will check your Appointment Confirmation and Passport.
  3. Token Generation: You will receive a token and wait in the seating area.
  4. Document Submission:
    • Verify DS-160 details with passport
    • Take and scan physical photos
    • Collect current and old passports
    • Organize documents into folder/envelope
  5. Acknowledgment: You will receive a stamped copy or receipt as proof of submission.

3. Essential Tips for 2026

  • Location Flexibility: Drop documents at any of the 5 major VACs (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata) for free.
  • Paid Drop-off: Available in cities like Bangalore, Pune, or Ahmedabad (Approx. 850–1,200 INR).
  • Post-Submission: CEAC status moves from "No Status" → "Ready" → "Issued" (7 to 21 days).
  • The 221(g) Risk: You may receive a Green/Yellow Slip requiring fingerprinting or interview.
