Why Parents B1/B2 Visas Get Rejected

How to Avoid Rejection (Proven Strategy)

REASON 1: Section 214(b) – “Possible Immigrant Intent”

What the officer thinks:

“These parents might stay back with their children.”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Saying: “We may stay longer if allowed”
  • Talking about future plans in the U.S.
  • Over-emphasizing children’s settlement

✅ How to Avoid:

Say clearly and consistently:

“This is a short personal visit only. We will return to India.”

Power Boost for Mr. Rao:

  • Mention permanent government job
  • Mention NOC
  • Mention return date

REASON 2: Weak Ties to India

Officer’s concern:

“What guarantees they will return?”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Not mentioning job or responsibilities
  • Saying children are the “only family”
  • No future plans mentioned in India

✅ How to Avoid:

Highlight at least 3 ties:

  • ✔ Permanent government employment
  • ✔ Family & relatives in India
  • ✔ Property / social responsibilities

Best Answer:

“Our home, family, and responsibilities are in India. I must return to duty.”

REASON 3: Over-Dependency on Children

Officer’s concern:

“Parents may become financially dependent.”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Saying children sponsor everything
  • Showing only children’s bank statements
  • Saying “they will take care of us”

✅ How to Avoid:

Golden Rule:

Parents pay → Children host

Safe Line:

“We are financially independent. Our sons are only providing accommodation.”

REASON 4: Vague or Flexible Travel Plan

Officer’s concern:

“No fixed plan means flexible stay.”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • “Around one month”
  • “Maybe 1–2 months”
  • “Depends on visa validity”

✅ How to Avoid:

Be specific and firm:

“We will stay for three weeks.”

REASON 5: Inconsistent Answers Between Spouses

Officer’s concern:

“Their stories don’t match.”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Different stay duration
  • Different funding answers
  • Different return plans

✅ How to Avoid:

Both must say the same:

  • Duration: 3 weeks
  • Purpose: Family visit
  • Return: Together

REASON 6: Saying Too Much (Over-Explanation)

Officer’s concern:

“Why are they justifying so much?”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Long emotional stories
  • Explaining children’s jobs in detail
  • Giving documents without being asked

✅ How to Avoid:

  • Short answers (1–2 sentences)
  • Calm and confident tone
  • Speak only when asked

REASON 7: Talking About Visa Extensions or Status Change

Officer’s concern:

“They already plan to overstay.”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Asking about extension rules
  • Mentioning B2 → H4 conversion
  • Asking about Green Card eligibility

✅ How to Avoid:

Never mention extensions or future visas.

REASON 8: Poor Interview Behavior

Officer’s concern:

“Lack of confidence = hidden intent.”

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Nervous explanations
  • Interrupting officer
  • Looking at spouse for answers

✅ How to Avoid:

  • Answer independently
  • Maintain eye contact
  • Calm, respectful tone
