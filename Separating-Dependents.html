Separating Dependents for Kolkata Priority Slots

Follow these steps to move grandparents into an individual/couple profile to access earlier interview dates.

To separate your grandparents from your current appointment group so they can take advantage of the Kolkata Parents Priority slots, you will need to "remove" them as dependents from your primary profile and then create or update their own individual/couple profile.

Technical Limitation: Because the Parents Priority category is Post-specific (Kolkata only), it usually cannot be selected if they remain part of a larger group that is looking for general appointments in other cities like Hyderabad or Chennai.

1 Remove Them from the Current Group

  • Log in to your primary account on usvisascheduling.com.
  • Navigate to the "Manage Dependents" or "Manage Group" section on the dashboard.
  • Locate your grandfather and grandmother in the list of dependents.
  • Click the "Remove" or "Delete" button next to their names.

Note: This does not delete their data or their paid fees; it simply unlinks them from your primary profile.

2 Create/Update a Separate Profile

Once removed from your group, they need to be the "Primary" applicants in their own application.

  • If they have an email login: Log into that specific account.
  • If they don’t have one: Create a new profile for your grandfather. Once logged in, add your grandmother as his only dependent.

3 Select Category and Location

In the new profile, you must follow this specific path to see the priority slots:

  • Select Post: Choose Kolkata.
  • Visa Category: Select Non-Immigrant Visa.
  • Visa Priority: Look for "Parents visiting children with legal status".
  • Self-Correction: Ensure they meet the 50+ age requirement. Since they are grandparents, they likely exceed this, and the system will validate this based on their DS-160.
  • Payment: When you reach the payment section, you shouldn't have to pay again. Enter the MRV Receipt Number from their previous payment. The system should recognize the receipt since it is tied to their Passport Numbers.

4. Why You Should Separate Them

  • Availability: General B1/B2 slots for groups are currently booking into late 2026. By separating them, they can likely get an interview in May or June 2026, well before the August 16 wedding.
  • Different Purposes: While remaing others might be attending a wedding (General Tourism), your grandparents qualify for a specific Priority Category that you do not. The system doesn't allow a single group to mix "Priority" and "General" applicants.

Important Note for the Wedding

Since the wedding is in August 2026, you (the general group) may still face a wait time for an interview that exceeds that date. Once your grandparents have their priority appointment confirmed, you can try to use their confirmed visa or appointment as a reason to request an Expedited Appointment (Emergency) for the rest of the family, citing the specific family event (the marriage), though these requests are not guaranteed.
