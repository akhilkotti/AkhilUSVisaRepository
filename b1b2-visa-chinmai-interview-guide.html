Biggest Mistakes That Cause B1/B2 Visa Rejection

❌ 1. Giving long explanations

Visa officers prefer short answers.

Bad example:

Actually my cousin is getting married and we planned many things…

Good example:

I am going to attend my cousin’s wedding.

❌ 2. Answer not matching DS-160

Your answers must exactly match your DS-160 form.

❌ 3. Saying “I will explore job opportunities”

This is a direct rejection reason.

Never say:

  • job search
  • work opportunities
  • networking for jobs

❌ 4. Showing intention to stay longer

Never say:

  • I might extend my stay
  • I may stay longer

Say:

I will stay for about two weeks.

❌ 5. Weak ties to India

The officer must see strong reasons to return:

  • Job
  • Family
  • Career

❌ 6. Nervous behavior

Avoid:

  • Over-explaining
  • Arguing
  • Talking too much

❌ 7. Wrong or inconsistent documents

Bring:

  • DS-160 confirmation
  • Appointment confirmation
  • Passport
  • Wedding invitation
  • Employment letter
  • Salary slips
  • Bank statement

Top 10 Reasons Indian B1/B2 Visas Get Rejected

Most rejections fall under Section 214(b) of U.S. immigration law, which means the officer is not convinced the applicant will return to their home country.

Weak Ties to India

This is the most common reason.

Strong ties include:

  • Stable job
  • Family in India
  • Property/business
  • Career commitments

Weak ties example:

  • Unemployed
  • Recently resigned job
  • No clear reason to return

Inconsistent Answers with DS-160

If your answers do not match your DS-160 form, the officer may reject the visa.

Unclear Purpose of Travel

Bad answer:

I just want to see the US.

Good answer:

I am traveling to attend my cousin’s wedding on August 16 and stay for about two weeks.

Suspicion of Immigration Intent

Never say:

  • I want to explore job opportunities
  • I might stay longer

Weak Financial Situation

Strong proof:

  • Bank statements
  • Salary slips
  • Sponsor details

Nervous or Over-Talking During Interview

Common mistakes:

  • Talking too much
  • Giving unnecessary details
  • Appearing confused

Poor Travel History (Sometimes)

This does not automatically cause rejection if other factors are strong.

Incorrect or Suspicious Documents

  • Fake bank statements
  • Fake employment letters
  • Incorrect invitation letters

Not Being Honest

Examples:

  • Hiding relatives in the US
  • Giving incorrect information
  • Changing answers

Poor Communication or Lack of Confidence

The officer may doubt the travel purpose if the applicant:

  • Cannot explain their travel plan
  • Appears very nervous
  • Gives confusing answers
